Engineer's Note: The Main Logic

Just like we organize files on a computer, scientists organize plants. The biggest "folder" split is based on Vascular Tissues (維管組織).

Xylem & Phloem (木質部 & 韌皮部)

Think of these as the "plumbing pipes" of the plant. They transport water and food. If a plant has them, it can grow tall. If not, it stays tiny.

Non-flowering Plants No flowers, reproduces by spores or naked seeds
Flowering Plants Has flowers, seeds inside fruit

The 4 Major Groups

Mosses (苔蘚)
  • No true roots, stems, leaves
  • No vascular tissue
  • Reproduce by Spores
  • Needs wet places
HK Location: Damp walls in wet markets, shady stream banks.
Ferns (蕨類)
  • Has roots, stems, leaves
  • Has vascular tissue
  • Reproduce by Spores
  • Leaves rolled up when young
HK Location: Hiking trails (e.g., Sword Fern).
Gymnosperms (裸子植物)
  • Has roots, stems, needle leaves
  • No flowers
  • Seeds in Cones (Naked)
HK Location: Buddhist Pine (羅漢松) in gardens.
Angiosperms (被子植物)
  • Has roots, stems, leaves
  • Has Flowers
  • Seeds inside Fruits
HK Location: Bauhinia (Golden Bauhinia Square).
