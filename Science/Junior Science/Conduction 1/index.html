The Particle Theory

Imagine you are standing in a crowded MTR train during rush hour. If one person starts pushing, they bump into the next person, who bumps into the next.

Conduction works the same way! When particles are heated, they gain Kinetic Energy. They vibrate faster and bump into their neighbors, passing the energy along.

Conductors vs. Insulators

Good Conductors

Materials that allow heat to flow through them easily. Usually metals.

Copper Aluminum Iron Gold

Why? Metals have "free electrons" that help carry energy faster!

Insulators (Poor Conductors)

Materials that stop or slow down heat transfer. Useful for handles and clothing.

Plastic Wood Air Glass

Why? Particles are held tightly in place and there are no free electrons.
