What is Convection?

Convection is the transfer of thermal energy through the movement of fluids (liquids and gases). Remember the golden rule: Hot fluid rises, and cold fluid sinks.

1. Heating

When a fluid is heated, particles move faster and move further apart (Expansion). The fluid becomes less dense.

2. Rising & Sinking

Less dense hot fluid rises (floats). The cooler, denser fluid sinks to take its place. This creates a circular motion called a Convection Current.

Why are Air Cons placed high?

In Hong Kong homes, AC units are installed near the ceiling. Why?

  • The AC blows out cold air.
  • Cold air is denser and sinks down.
  • Hot air rises to the top to be cooled by the AC.
  • This creates a cycle that cools the whole room efficiently.

Boiling Water

When cooking instant noodles, the heat source is at the bottom.

  • Water at the bottom gets hot, expands, and rises.
  • Cooler water at the top sinks to the bottom.
  • Eventually, all the water boils.
