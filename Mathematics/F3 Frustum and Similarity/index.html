The "Subtraction" Strategy

A Frustum is made by cutting a smaller cone (or pyramid) from the top of a larger one.

\( V_{frustum} = V_{large} - V_{small} \)

Key Property: The small cone and large cone are Similar Figures. Their dimensions are proportional.

\( \frac{r}{R} = \frac{h}{H} \)

History Corner

Liu Hui & The Nine Chapters

Ancient Chinese mathematicians mastered volume calculations centuries ago.

The "Shang Gong" (Construction) Chapter

In the mathematical classic The Nine Chapters on the Mathematical Art (九章算術), written around the 1st century AD, there is a dedicated chapter called "Shang Gong" (Consultations on Construction).

The famous mathematician Liu Hui (劉徽) (3rd century AD) provided detailed commentaries. He used a method called dissection to calculate volumes.

Square Frustum
Fangting (方亭)

A pavilion with a square base. Liu Hui broke this shape down into a central block and wedges.

Circular Frustum
Yuanting (圓亭)

Similar to the cone frustum in our tool. The book provided precise volume formulas.
