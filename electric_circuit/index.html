Objective: Learn about circuit symbols, current flow, and voltage measurements in electric circuits.

  1. Drag components from the palette to build your circuit
  2. Connect components by placing them close to each other
  3. Adjust voltage and resistance to observe changes in current
  4. Observe the direction of current flow (conventional) and electron flow
  5. Use ammeter and voltmeter to measure current and voltage
  6. Record data points to analyze the relationship between V, I, and R
Key Concepts:
  • Current (I): Flow of electric charge, measured in Amperes (A)
  • Voltage (V): Electrical potential difference, measured in Volts (V)
  • Resistance (R): Opposition to current flow, measured in Ohms (Œ©)
  • Ohm's Law: V = I √ó R (Voltage equals Current times Resistance)
  • Conventional Current: Flows from positive to negative (red arrows)
  • Electron Flow: Flows from negative to positive (blue dots)
